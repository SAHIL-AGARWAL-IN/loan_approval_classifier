Model Mathematical Blueprint

The model operates on 28 standardized features including engineered interaction parameters:

$$ \text{LogOdds}(z) = \beta_0 + \sum_{i=1}^{28} \beta_i \left( \frac{X_i - \mu_i}{\sigma_i} \right) $$ $$ P(\text{Approved}) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-z}} $$

To fit the non-linear thresholds of loan approvals, two critical feature engineering transformations are applied:

  • Credit Score Squared ($Credit\_Score^2$): Dramatically accelerates the approval probability as credit ratings cross key tiers (e.g. from subprime to prime).
  • DTI Ratio Squared ($DTI\_Ratio^2$): Imposes an exponential penalty for borrowers as debt exceeds comfortable margins.
  • Log Income ($\ln(Income + 1)$): Compresses wide income distributions to represent diminishing returns on extreme wealth.

Architectural Rationale

Logistic Regression was chosen for deployment because it shows the highest Recall (83.6%) and F1-Score (81.0%). In credit lending, missing out on eligible borrowers (false negatives) is expensive, so higher recall with solid precision is preferred.

KNN suffers due to the high dimensionality (28 features after one-hot encoding) causing distance metrics to collapse (curse of dimensionality). Naive Bayes performs well but assumes independence of features, which is strongly violated by standard one-hot encoded variables.

About LendOptima AI

LendOptima AI is an interactive machine learning classification dashboard designed to analyze and predict loan eligibility in real time.

It utilizes a client-side Logistic Regression model (achieving 88.0% accuracy, 83.6% recall, and 81.0% F1-score) trained on applicant metrics. The model integrates key financial features along with engineered interaction metrics—such as $Credit\_Score^2$ and $DTI\_Ratio^2$—to evaluate credit worthiness dynamically.

Adjust applicant financial attributes in the Eligibility Predictor to run predictions in real-time and explore model feature weights directly.
